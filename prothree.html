Overview

Technology is constantly advancing and developing while revolving around the concept of human-centered design; however, one in particular, vastly and significantly impacts the digital design field and expertise, a drawing tablet. A drawing tablet is a type of digital art board that connects to a system (computer) that allows individuals to write or draw on the tablet surface with a stylus, and have the information transferred and displayed onto a screen. One of the most common drawing tablets purchased and used currently is the Wacom drawing tablet. Masahiko Yamada, founder of Wacom, established the company on July 12, 1983 and strove to further the creative process and expand the compatibility of devices with universal programs that utilized the same inking language, while providing a “variety of graphical tools and features.” Despite the Wacom tablet seeming to have originated from a pair of devices (tablet and stylus), it actually began as a sole product.
